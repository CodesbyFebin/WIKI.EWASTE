## Kerala E-Waste Landscape

### Statistics
- Kerala state e-waste generation: 180,000 MT annually
- Per capita generation: ~5.5 kg per person (highest in India)
- Formal recycling rate: ~8% (below national average)
- Informal sector: ~92% of e-waste processed without compliance

### KSPCB Role
- Enforces CPCB national e-waste rules at state level
- Maintains list of authorized recyclers
- Conducts facility inspections (quarterly minimum)
- Issues penalties for non-compliance
- Coordinates with Kochi Municipal Corporation

## Key Compliance Requirements in Kerala

### 1. Authorized Recycler List
- KSPCB maintains updated list at kspcb.kerala.gov.in
- Only 15-20 authorized recyclers across Kerala
- Most concentrated in Kochi and Thiruvananthapuram
- Verification required before partnership

### 2. Collection Targets
- Producers must register with KSPCB (not just CPCB)
- Annual collection targets set by KSPCB
- Quarterly reporting to KSPCB portal
- Non-compliance fine: INR 50,000+ per quarter

### 3. Transportation Rules
- Hazmat permits required for inter-state e-waste movement
- Kochi port city facilitates international export (post-processing)
- Documentation: Manifest, insurance, driver credentials
- Route approval from KSPCB Hazmat Cell

### 4. Storage & Facility Standards
- Facilities must comply with Kerala Building Code
- Fire-rated storage (minimum 2-hour rating)
- Pollution control certificate (from KSPCB directly)
- Annual environmental audit mandatory

## KSPCB Inspection Checklist

When KSPCB conducts facility audits, they verify:

- [ ] Authorization certificate (current, not expired)
- [ ] ISO 14001 certification
- [ ] Worker safety equipment (PPE, fire extinguishers)
- [ ] Temperature monitoring systems
- [ ] Wastewater treatment (if hydrometallurgy)
- [ ] Air emission monitoring (if incineration)
- [ ] Record-keeping (collection, processing, material recovery)
- [ ] Pest control and hygiene standards
- [ ] Security and perimeter fencing
- [ ] Segregation of hazardous materials

**Penalty Structure for Violations**:
- Minor violation: INR 10,000-50,000 (warning required first)
- Major violation: INR 50,000-5 Lakh
- Repeated violation: License suspension (6-12 months)
- Criminal violation: INR 1+ Crore + imprisonment

## Kochi-Specific Guidance

### Authorized Recyclers in Kochi Area
1. **EWasteKochi** - Hydrometallurgy (cobalt, nickel recovery)
2. **Kerala Recycling Industries** - Mechanical shredding
3. **Cochin E-Waste Solutions** - Mixed processing

### Collection Points
- Kochi Municipal Corporation (Fort Kochi office)
- Infopark Kochi (IT sector collection)
- Kakkanad IT Park (collection point)
- KSPCB regional office (direct drop-off)

### Transport Routes
- Kochi-Trivandrum highway: Well-established
- Port facility: For international export post-processing
- Intra-city: Hazmat permits required >10 MT

## Filing KSPCB Compliance Reports

**Online Portal**: kspcb.kerala.gov.in (E-Waste Management Portal)

**Quarterly Report Format**:
```
Reporting Organization: [Name]
KSPCB License #: [License Number]
Quarter: Q1 2026

E-Waste Collection (January-March 2026):
- Total units collected: [X]
- Total weight (kg): [Y]

Authorized Recycler Used:
- Name: [Recycler Name]

Material Recovery Summary:

```

## Cost Impact for Kochi Organizations

- **KSPCB Annual Registration**: INR 5,000-10,000
- **Authorized Recycler Premium**: +INR 100-200/unit (vs. national average)
- **Compliance Audits**: INR 20,000-50,000 annually
- **Transportation (Kochi-based)**: INR 100-300/unit (shorter distances favorable)

**Advantage**: Kochi's established recycling ecosystem makes compliance easier than many Indian cities.
