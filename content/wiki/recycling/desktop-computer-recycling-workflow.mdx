## Desktop Computer Anatomy

A typical desktop computer contains:
- **Motherboard**: Copper, gold, silver traces (~0.1% precious metal content)
- **Power Supply**: Copper coils, aluminum heat sinks
- **Case**: Steel or aluminum structure
- **RAM**: Gold connectors, copper circuit traces
- **Storage**: Aluminum platters (HDD) or silicon (SSD)
- **Monitor/Peripherals**: Various hazardous materials

## Recycling Process

### Step 1: Assessment & Segregation
- Power on and backup data (if functional)
- Document condition and components
- Separate functioning vs. scrap units

### Step 2: Data Sanitization
- Wipe drives per NIST 800-88
- Remove and destroy any physical storage if needed
- Obtain sanitization certificate

### Step 3: Disassembly
- Remove monitor and peripherals
- Disconnect power supply
- Extract RAM, storage, copper components
- Separate ferrous (steel) from non-ferrous (aluminum, copper)

### Step 4: Material Processing
- Copper recovery: 2-3 kg per desktop (value ~INR 20-30)
- Aluminum recovery: 1-2 kg per desktop (value ~INR 2.50-5)
- Precious metals: ~0.05 kg (value ~INR 100-200)

### Step 5: Final Disposition
- Remaining plastic: Recycled or incinerated
- Hazardous waste: Safely disposed by CPCB-authorized facility
- Material recovery report generated

## Economics

- **Recycling cost**: INR 800-1,200 per unit
- **Material recovery value**: INR 300-600 per unit
- **Net cost to organization**: INR 200-900 per unit
- **Environmental benefit**: 35 kg CO2e avoided vs. landfill
