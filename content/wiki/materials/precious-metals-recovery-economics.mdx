## Recovery Value Per Device

### Scenario 1: 1,000 Desktop Computers
- Average weight per unit: 3 kg
- Precious metals content: ~2 kg per 1,000 units
  - Gold: 0.2 kg (value: INR 1.3 lakh)
  - Copper: 25 kg (value: INR 22.5 lakh)
- **Total recoverable value**: INR 23.8 lakh
- **Recovery cost**: INR 15 lakh (collection, processing, refining)
- **Net value**: INR 8.8 lakh

### Scenario 2: Mixed E-Waste (1,000 units including laptops, monitors, peripherals)
- Average precious metal content higher: ~3.5 kg per 1,000 units
- **Total recoverable value**: INR 35 lakh
- **Recovery cost**: INR 18 lakh (complex sorting, mixed materials)
- **Net value**: INR 17 lakh

## Business Models

### Model 1: In-House Recovery (Capital-Intensive)
- **Investment**: INR 1-2 crore (equipment, facility, staff)
- **Throughput**: 50-100 MT/year
- **Revenue**: INR 50-100 lakh annually
- **Profitability**: High (for scale)
- **Challenges**: Regulatory compliance, hazardous waste management

### Model 2: Partner with Authorized Recycler (Operational Model)
- **Investment**: INR 10-20 lakh (equipment for collection, storage)
- **Throughput**: 100-500 MT/year
- **Revenue**: INR 30-80 lakh annually (material recovery credits)
- **Profitability**: Moderate (revenue sharing with recycler)
- **Advantages**: Compliance-ready, no capital burden

### Model 3: Supply to Global Recycler (Logistics Model)
- **Investment**: INR 5-10 lakh (collection, sorting, export documentation)
- **Throughput**: 500+ MT/year
- **Revenue**: INR 50-200 lakh annually (bulk material sales)
- **Profitability**: High margins but subject to global commodity fluctuations
