## EPR Overview

Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) shifts end-of-life management costs from municipalities to manufacturers and importers of electronic products.

## Who Must Comply?

- Manufacturers of electronic equipment (domestic)
- Importers of electronic equipment
- Retailers (in some states)
- **Exemptions**: Small-scale or home-based operations (varies by state)

## Key Obligations

### 1. Registration
- Register with CPCB within 30 days of operations
- Renew annually by March 31
- Fee: INR 5,000-20,000 per registration

### 2. Collection Targets
- Target setting: CPCB issues annual collection targets
- Typical ranges: 10-50% of annual sales by weight
- Reporting: Quarterly to state PCBs

### 3. Financial Responsibility
- EPR fees: INR 100-300 per device (negotiable)
- Payment to authorized recyclers
- Documentation of all transactions

### 4. Record Keeping
- Maintain 5-year documentation
- Audit-ready certificate archive
- Material recovery reports

## Quarterly Reporting

**Required Data**:
- Total units sold
- Collection volume (units and weight)
- Recycler list and authorization numbers
- Material recovery summary
- Financial transactions

**Reporting Portal**: CPCB's E-Waste Management System (cpcb.nic.in)

## Common Penalties

- Missing targets: INR 50,000+ per MT shortfall
- Late registration: INR 50,000-1 Crore
- Non-compliance: Up to INR 1 Crore + imprisonment
