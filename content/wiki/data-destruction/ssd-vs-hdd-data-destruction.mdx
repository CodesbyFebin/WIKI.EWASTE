## HDD vs SSD: Data Storage Fundamentals

### HDD (Hard Disk Drive)
- **Technology**: Magnetic storage on rotating platters
- **Data Location**: Specific sectors with known addresses
- **Erasure**: Overwriting accessible sectors effective
- **Recovery Risk**: High (forensics can recover overwritten data)

### SSD (Solid State Drive)
- **Technology**: Flash memory cells (NAND chips)
- **Data Location**: Wear-leveling spreads writes; some cells may be inaccessible
- **Erasure**: Overwriting all accessible sectors may leave "hidden" data on reserved cells
- **Recovery Risk**: Extremely high (reserved cells not addressed by standard commands)

## Data Destruction Methods Comparison

| Method | HDD | SSD | NIST 800-88 Compliant |
|--------|-----|-----|----------------------|
| **Clear (Logical)** | Low risk | High risk | No |
| **Overwrite** | Acceptable | Risky (reserved cells) | Conditional |
| **ATA Secure Erase** | Effective | Unreliable | Conditional |
| **Cryptographic Erase** | Acceptable | Effective | Yes |
| **Degaussing** | Effective | Ineffective | HDD only |
| **Physical Destruction** | Most assurance | Most assurance | Yes |

## Recommended Methods

### For HDD
1. **Primary**: ATA Secure Erase (fast, vendor-verified)
2. **Secondary**: DoD 5220.22-M overwrite (3-7 passes)
3. **Maximum**: Physical destruction (shredding)

### For SSD
1. **Primary**: Cryptographic Erasure (if encrypted beforehand)
2. **Secondary**: Manufacturer's Secure Erase (if available & verified)
3. **Maximum**: Physical Destruction (shredding, crushing)

## Cost Implications

- **HDD Overwrite**: INR 500-800 per drive
- **SSD Cryptographic Erase**: INR 600-1,000 per drive
- **Physical Destruction**: INR 1,500-3,000 per drive
- **Mixed environment (80% HDD, 20% SSD)**: Recommend physical destruction as standard (avg INR 1,000/drive)
